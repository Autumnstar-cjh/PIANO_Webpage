Abstract

Faster, physics-consistent
coronal field reconstruction

Solar magnetic field extrapolation is essential for understanding flares and coronal mass ejections. Traditional NLFFF solvers rely on iterative numerical methods that are computationally expensive and difficult to apply in real-time. PIANO learns a direct mapping from boundary observations to the full 3D coronal field — achieving state-of-the-art accuracy at a fraction of the cost.

PIANO supports multimodal input, combining image-based boundary data with physical scalar parameters, and integrates attention mechanisms to fuse this information effectively.

To ensure physical plausibility, divergence-free and force-free constraints are incorporated directly into training. A two-phase strategy further improves volumetric consistency throughout the predicted field.

Architecture

How PIANO works

Fig. 2 — PIANO architecture: multimodal lifting, Fourier neural operator layers, and physics-constrained projection.

00
The Core Idea
PIANO maps boundary magnetic field data Bx(x,y), By(x,y), Bz(x,y) directly to a volumetric 3D field B(x,y,z) — replacing expensive iterative solvers with a single learned forward pass, without sacrificing physical consistency.
01
Multimodal Input Lifting
Observed magnetic field is lifted into high-dimensional feature space via MLP. Scalar physical parameters (domain lengths) are processed in parallel and fused with the image features using a lightweight attention mechanism.
02
Fourier Neural Operator Layers
Features pass through Fourier layers operating in frequency space, capturing global spatial interactions and long-range magnetic field dependencies at low computational cost.
03
Physics-Informed Training
Two penalties are enforced during training: a divergence-free condition (∇·B = 0) and a force-free condition (J × B = 0), ensuring predictions remain physically plausible.
04
Two-Phase Training Strategy
Phase one trains on 2D boundary data with physics constraints. Phase two retrains using the model's own 3D predictions as input, progressively refining volumetric consistency.

Results

Component-wise comparison
across heights 2–16 Mm

Predicted field components and error maps compared across models at heights 2–16 Mm. Errors concentrate near the lower boundary and diminish with height. PIANO shows particular strength at higher altitudes, especially for the structurally complex By component.

Bx Horizontal · x-axis

Most neural operator models achieve accurate extrapolation for Bx, with errors concentrated near lower heights (2–4 Mm). Prediction accuracy improves as height increases, reflecting smoother magnetic structures away from the boundary.

By Horizontal · y-axis

The By structure is more complex, resulting in larger and more persistent errors across heights. PIANO maintains stronger accuracy at higher heights, demonstrating clearer structural consistency compared to other models.

Bz Vertical · z-axis

Similar to Bx, most models achieve strong reconstruction for Bz. Error magnitude decreases with height, indicating improved agreement with ground truth in upper layers.
